# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

International, high-profile business-aviation clients — corporate flight departments, HNW private travelers, aircraft owners, and prospective pilots/technicians — evaluating a Belgrade-based operator. They arrive expecting proof of safety, expertise, and discretion within seconds, often on mobile, sometimes under time pressure (an AOG event). A secondary audience is talent: aspiring pilots and aviation technicians researching the academy and technical training.

## Product Purpose

A single, cinematic landing experience that immediately communicates who Prince Aviation is and its four business pillars — Private Charter, MRO / Aircraft Maintenance, Pilot Academy, Technical Training — replacing a sprawling, over-complex legacy site with one clear, premium narrative. Success: a first-time visitor understands the four pillars and reaches the segment they need within one scroll, and an AOG contact is never more than one tap away.

## Positioning

The full lifecycle of business aviation under one roof in Belgrade — fly, maintain, and train to a single uncompromising standard.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary CTA: Request a charter / contact the relevant pillar. Secondary CTA: AOG 24/7 support (phone + WhatsApp), always reachable.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Four disciplines. One standard."
- Belief ladder: (1) This is a serious, safety-first operator, not a broker. (2) They own the whole chain — flight, maintenance, training — so nothing is outsourced. (3) They operate at an international premium standard. (4) If something goes wrong, they respond now. → therefore I can trust them with my aircraft / my flight / my career.
- Proof on hand: real fleet + facility photography in `Galerija/` (twilight hangar with illuminated signage, jets at golden hour, in-house maintenance, training aircraft). Serbian registrations YU-FVJ, YU-SPC, YU-SVL, YU-BRI, YU-TCA. Belgrade base. No fabricated statistics or certifications until the client supplies verified figures.

## Brand Personality

Precise, ascendant, quietly powerful. Speaks like an experienced captain: calm, exact, never loud. Confidence through restraint and craft, not adjectives. Luxury read through atmosphere and photography rather than ornament.

## Anti-references

The navy-and-gold luxury-aviation monoculture. Generic broker marketplaces. Cluttered multi-page legacy structures with deep menus. Stocky "corporate handshake" imagery. Loud, over-animated fade-on-scroll-everything pages. Editorial display-serif magazine affectation.

## Design Principles

- **Show the real aircraft.** Their own fleet and facility photography carries the page; no generic stock, no colored placeholder blocks.
- **One standard, one narrative.** The whole site reads as a single confident story, not a menu of pages.
- **Motion that means flight.** The signature scroll-driven takeoff and flight-path are the identity, not decoration — every other section stays calm around it.
- **Crimson is the signature.** The brand's red mark is the differentiator against navy-and-gold; commit to it, let gold stay a thread.
- **AOG is sacred.** Emergency contact is always one tap away, on every viewport.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 on the dark canvas, visible focus states, full keyboard paths, real semantics and alt text. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative (static hero, no pin, instant reveals). Touch targets ≥44px; AOG actions reachable without hover.
